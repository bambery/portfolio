Illinois/Indiana Girl Scout Council Online Store
After eight Girl Scout councils merged into one large council spanning large portions of Illinois and Indiana, changing needs and a growing Spanish-language demographic called for a new approach for handling access to programming and materials. As part of the UX analysis, some of the actions I performed included:
  1. Interviewed program managers, troop leaders, volunteers, parents, and storefront workers to document interactions with the council store and website and catalog pain points
  2. Performed card sorts with troop leaders, parents to restructure site contents and store navigation to meet user's mental models and reduce friction
  3. Constructed data reports combined with interview responses to develop distinct personas representing customer groups
  4. Documented complex manual processes for store management and order fulfillment to provide automation recommendations
  5. Analyzed, documented, translated, and consolidated four isolated legacy data stores into one PSQL reporting database
  6. Provided recommendations for SEO, content findability, navigation changes, and URL consolidation
  7. PCI/DSS and security analysis
  8. Cost/benefit/risk analysis for next steps.
